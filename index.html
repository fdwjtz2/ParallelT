    Abstract:

      Text-to-speech (TTS) aims to synthesize speech with proper prosody of reply based on the input text sequence. Most TTS systems consider semantic features and acoustic features at the text coding layer to enhance prosodic expression, but the gap between text and speech still affects the quality of synthesized speech. In this paper, we propose Parallel-TTS, which combines end-to-end multi-modal speech synthesis with two-stage speech synthesis based on pre-trained models, using Parallel Tokens that contain both semantic and acoustic information to guide speech synthesis. We also extend the proposed method to zero-shot learning. Both objective and subjective evaluation results show the effectiveness of the proposed method.

    Zero-Shot Multi-Speaker TTS

  • We divide the VCTK dataset into Train, Development (containing the same speakers as the train set) and Test.
  • For the test set, we selected 11 speakers that are neither in the development nor the training set; following the proposal by other papers, we selected 1 representative from each accent totaling 7 women and 4 men (speakers 225, 234, 238, 245, 248, 261, 294, 302, 326, 335 and 347).
  • Based on results in single speaker TTS experiment, we choose the 6th layer of Wav2vec2.0 as the acoustic feature and 12th layer as the semantic feature and composite them as parallel tokens for Parallel-TTS.
